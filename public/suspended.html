Your AWS Account Has Been Suspended

Why was my account suspended?

Your AWS account has been temporarily suspended due to one or more of the following billing-related issues:

  • Outstanding payment balance on your account
  • Payment method declined, expired, or invalid
  • Billing information requires immediate update
  • Failure to resolve previous billing notifications

What happens during suspension?

While your account is suspended, the following restrictions apply:

  • Access to AWS Management Console is limited
  • Running services and resources may be stopped
  • New resource creation is disabled
  • API access may be restricted
  • Data retention policies remain in effect for a limited time

How to restore your account

To reactivate your AWS account and restore full access to your services, you must:

  • Sign in to the AWS Billing Console to review your account status
  • Update your payment method with valid billing information
  • Clear all outstanding balances on your account
  • Verify that your payment method can process the required charges
  • Wait for account verification (typically 1-2 hours after payment)

Important Notice

Failure to resolve billing issues within the specified timeframe may result in permanent account closure and data loss. Please take immediate action to regularize your payment and restore your account access.

For detailed billing information and payment options, please access the AWS Billing and Cost Management Console using your account credentials.
